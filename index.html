Timeline of Acharya Jagadish Chandra Bose

  • 1858 - Born on November 30 in Mymensingh (now in Bangladesh).
  • 1869 – Enrolled at Hare School in Kolkata.
  • 1875 – Completed school education and joined St. Xavier's College, Kolkata.
  • 1879 – Graduated with a degree in Physics from the University of Calcutta.
  • 1880 – Moved to England and joined Christ's College, Cambridge, to study Natural Sciences.
  • 1884 – Completed his B.Sc. from the University of London. Returned to India and joined Presidency College, Kolkata, as a professor of physics.
  • 1894 – Began pioneering research in radio waves and microwaves.
  • 1895 – Demonstrated the first wireless communication using radio waves, before Marconi.
  • 1899 – Presented his findings on millimeter waves to the Royal Institution in London.
  • 1901 – Discovered the electrical response of plants to stimuli, proving that plants have life.
  • 1917 – Established the Bose Institute in Kolkata for scientific research.
  • 1920 – Became the first Indian scientist to be elected a Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS), London.
  • 1937 – Passed away on November 23 in Giridih, Bihar (now in Jharkhand).
